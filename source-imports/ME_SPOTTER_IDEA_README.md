# Me!

Status: queued build after Hopper111 lockdown, Contacts rebuild, and the 24/7 local session runner.

Purpose:
Me! is a recipient-support tool for people receiving bullying, harassment, humiliation, or online cruelty. It helps them understand what is happening, separate the attack from their actual worth, choose a safe next step, and regain clarity, steadiness, and self-respect.

Core product direction:
- Validate first.
- Clarify what kind of harmful behavior is happening.
- Offer safe next-step options.
- Help the user recover their footing.
- Use lightness or humor only after the user feels seen.

Primary jobs:
- Identify bullying, harassment, baiting, humiliation, or manipulation.
- Help the user decide whether to ignore, block, report, document, set a boundary, or send a calm response.
- Protect self-esteem and awareness.
- Deflate cruelty without dragging the user into cruelty.

Relationship to Hopper111:
Ledger should eventually learn when Me! is the right intervention to offer in the field.

## Related Protection Lane: Spotter

Spotter is a separate but related parent-facing protection tool focused on grooming recognition, predatory targeting, coercion, manipulation, isolation, harmful online influence, self-harm risk, and abuse-risk around children and youth.

Icon direction:
Use the red binoculars app icon on a soft white rounded-square tile. The symbol should communicate watchfulness, early recognition, and parent readiness without making the product feel clinical, punitive, or surveillance-heavy.

Core promise:
- Help a parent, guardian, witness, or older youth recognize concerning patterns earlier.
- Preserve observations and evidence clearly.
- Explain danger in plain language.
- Route the user toward the safest next step quickly.

Spotter should be careful not to present itself as a legal, clinical, or law-enforcement authority. It should not make definitive accusations or claim to diagnose abuse. Its role is to support recognition, documentation, parent-child conversation, and safer routing.

Possible billable feature:

Local Safety Map / Local Help Packet

User enters ZIP code, city/state, or optional current location. Spotter returns local and official resources such as:

- state CPS reporting hotline
- county child protective services office where available
- local police non-emergency number
- sheriff department where county jurisdiction matters
- emergency reminder to call 911 if there is immediate danger
- official online reporting links where available
- state mandatory reporting guidance
- school or student-safety contacts when relevant
- child advocacy center links where available
- NCMEC / CyberTipline path for online exploitation concerns
- Childhelp hotline as a national fallback

Important product note:
Area code alone is not reliable enough for jurisdiction. Use ZIP code, city/state, optional current location, and questions like "Where did this happen?" or "Where does the child live?" when routing matters.

## Parent Conversation Methodology

Spotter should include a SayIt-style conversation helper for parents and guardians.

Purpose:
Help a scared parent talk to a child in a way that lowers shame, preserves safety, and keeps the child connected instead of pushing them deeper into secrecy or isolation.

This should not be generic advice. It should generate practical parent-facing language for the next sentence, next question, or next boundary.

Core principles:

- Calm first.
- Connection before correction.
- Safety before interrogation.
- Curiosity before accusation.
- Preserve evidence before deleting.
- Do not shame the child for what happened online.
- Do not mock ideology, loneliness, sexual confusion, fear, or embarrassment.
- Do not promise secrecy if safety or abuse reporting may be involved.
- Do not confront suspected adults, exploiters, or online actors in a way that could increase risk.

Example parent scripts:

- "I am not mad. I am here because I am worried about your safety."
- "You are not in trouble for telling me."
- "Whatever someone said or asked you to do online, we can handle it together."
- "You do not have to explain it perfectly. Start wherever you can."
- "I may need help from another safe adult, but I will not leave you alone in this."
- "I am going to stay calm. Your job is just to be honest with me one step at a time."

Conversation modes:

- Something feels wrong
- My child is hiding something
- My child may be getting groomed
- My child may be isolated or radicalized
- My child may be suicidal
- My child was threatened or blackmailed online
- My child sent or received sexual images
- I need to ask without making it worse
- I need to set a boundary tonight

Parent output should include:

- what to say first
- what not to say
- what to ask next
- what to document
- when to pause the conversation
- when to bring in crisis help, CPS, law enforcement, school safety, or a therapist

For isolation, extremist, incel-adjacent, coercive, or self-harm situations, use safer product language:

Isolation & Online Influence Safety Plan

Avoid using "incel extraction" in the product UI. The parent may understand the shorthand privately, but the product should frame the work as safety, connection, de-escalation, and recovery from harmful online influence.
